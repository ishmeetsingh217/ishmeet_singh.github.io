ADDRESSING THE CHALLENGES

 

Our team had good synergy throughout this project and helped each other overcome problems on the 
technical front. However, we struggled with consistency in design and formatting for the website, 
presentation, and wireframes. Since everyone has their ideas and methods of doing work this affected the 
overall product initially. 
 
Our original idea for the website was for everyone to make it on their accounts and we would import the 
pages into a coherent website. Even while using the same design template the minor details were just not 
lining up and ended up taking too long for everyone to change. 
 
Since we had the overall idea for the pages developed, we created a new iteration of the website where 

one person took everyone’s ideas and meshed them with one person’s design choices, so the website 

flowed together. We found this technique to work best because everything got a second glance over so 
nothing was overlooked. 
 
Another major problem with working in groups in general and exacerbated by online learning was 
scheduling. Since everyone is busy outside of class it was difficult to find one consistent meeting time 
every week for discussion on the progression of the project. Instead, we opted for an asynchronous 

approach where we outlined everyone’s goals for a week on a Google doc sheet and as each group 

member completed their task it was highlighted on the task to show completion. We then only needed 
group meetings bi-weekly where we would discuss the next step. This also coincides with the problem of 
sharing our work and discussing specific problems. 
 
Our main form of communication was through Facebook, but we found this difficult to ask for help from 
our partners. So, we used screen-sharing and call features with Zoom to make it easier to describe and 
show the problem in which we faced.
